RIVERS IN PAKISTAN

MAJOR RIVERS IN PAKISTAN

There are six Major Rivers in Pakistan:

  • INDUS
  • JHELUM
  • CHENAB
  • RAVI
  • SUTLEJ
  • KABUL

INDUS RIVER:

  1. Indus River or better known as “Sindhu” is a largest water body in South-East Asia.
  2. It is the longest river on the western side of the Indian subcontinent.
  3. Indus River has the total length of 2880 km and it is also a trans-boundary river.
  4. It flows through the Tibetan region of China, India and Pakistan.

JHELUM RIVER:

  1. Jhelum River is the largest and most western among the five rivers of Punjab.
  2. It passes through Jhelum district and it is a tributary of River Indus.
  3. The total length of Jhelum River is 725 km and the drainage area is 15,856 square km.

CHENAB RIVER:

  1. Chenab River is a transboundary river that flows in both India and Pakistan.
  2. Chenab River originates from Himachal Pradesh and travels to Jammu and Kashmir.
  3. From J & K, it enters Pakistan and meets the Indus River before going into the Arabian Sea.

RAVI RIVER:

  1. This river is the smallest of all five rivers that flow in Punjab.
  2. It traces its course through India and Pakistan.
  3. Ravi River is an important river of the Indus water system.

SUTLEJ RIVER:

  1. The Sutlej or Satluj River is the longest of the five rivers that flow through the historic crossroads region of Punjab in northern India and Pakistan.

KABUL RIVER:

  1. River that emerges in the Sanglakh Range of the Hindu Kush mountains in the northeastern part of Maidan Wardak Province, Afghanistan.
